About Me

Hi, I’m Sakshi.

I overthink interfaces. I notice when something feels slightly off. I get curious about why a flow feels smooth in one app and exhausting in another. That curiosity is what pushed me into design, not the aesthetics, not the trends, but the psychology behind it.

I’m fascinated by the invisible layer of products. The assumptions. The tradeoffs. The quiet decisions someone made about what a user “should” understand. I’m the person who will pause mid-scroll and wonder who decided that button belonged there, and what conversation led to it.

I’m currently studying Information Experience Design at Pratt, but most of my real growth has come from building things outside of obligation. I don’t like waiting for permission to experiment. If I want to understand something, I’ll prototype it. If I’m curious about AI, I’ll build with it. If I think there’s a better way to structure a system, I’ll map it out until it makes sense.

I’ve worked on everything from AI legal workspaces to music streaming loyalty systems to research-heavy usability studies. What connects all of it isn’t the industry — it’s the complexity. I’m drawn to problems that feel layered, slightly overwhelming at first glance. I like untangling them.

I use AI daily, but not because it’s fashionable. I use it because I’m ambitious about how I think. I want to explore more directions before committing. I want to pressure-test my logic. I want to catch blind spots early. AI helps me move through ambiguity faster, but it doesn’t make decisions for me. I’m too skeptical for that. I need to understand why something works, not just that it does.

Outside of work, I’m deeply rooted in where I come from. I’m Maharashtrian, and I carry that with me in how I live and how I design. I care about family, about building something meaningful, about not losing warmth while chasing ambition. I romanticize cooking elaborate meals, long conversations, small celebrations. I think that balance matters.

I don’t want to just “ship features.” I want to build systems that feel thoughtful. I want to work on products that reduce confusion instead of adding to it. I want to grow into someone who doesn’t just design screens, but shapes direction.

I’m still early in my career, and I’m aware of that. But I’m intentional. I’m hungry. And I’m not interested in doing shallow work.

If you’re here, you’re probably trying to understand who I am beyond the projects. The simplest answer is this: I care deeply about clarity, I’m obsessed with improving things that feel messy, and I’m building toward something bigger than just a job title.

And I’m just getting started.
