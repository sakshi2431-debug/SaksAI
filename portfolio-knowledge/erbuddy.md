## Overview

ERbuddy is a healthcare communication platform designed to improve coordination between elderly patients and their caregivers. The product focuses on reducing confusion around daily care, medications, exercises, and health updates by centralizing information and enabling clear, two-way communication.

The goal of this project was to design an intuitive, accessible mobile experience that supports real-world caregiving scenarios while accommodating users with varying levels of technical comfort.

## Problem Statement

Patients and caregivers often struggle with fragmented health information, unclear responsibilities, and poor communication channels. Existing tools are either too complex or fail to address the emotional and practical realities of caregiving.

ERbuddy needed a redesigned experience that:
- Centralizes essential health information
- Supports daily caregiving workflows
- Is accessible and easy to use for elderly users

---

## Design Goals

1. **Centralize Health Information**  
   Ensure critical patient data (exercises, logs, schedules) is easy to access and understand.

2. **Enable Clear Two-Way Communication**  
   Allow patients and caregivers to stay aligned through shared logs, reminders, and updates.

3. **Improve Usability & Accessibility**  
   Design for clarity, simplicity, and accessibility across age groups and abilities.

---

## Research & Discovery

### Methods Used
- Desk research on caregiving and elderly health management
- Competitive analysis of healthcare and caregiver apps
- Audit of ERbuddy’s existing information architecture
- Review of current task flows, including:
  - Onboarding
  - Editing health profiles
  - Medication reminders
  - Doctor appointment scheduling

### Key Insights
- Users prefer fewer steps and clearer language
- Overloaded dashboards increase anxiety
- Caregivers need quick status visibility without digging through data

---

## Information Architecture & Task Flows

Based on research findings:
- The information architecture was simplified and restructured
- Core user journeys were mapped end-to-end
- Task flows were created for critical actions such as:
  - Daily check-ins
  - Exercise tracking
  - Logbook updates
  - Schedule review

Collaborative sketching sessions helped align the team before moving into wireframes.

---

## Wireframing & Ideation

- Low- to mid-fidelity wireframes were created to test layout, hierarchy, and flow
- Designs focused on:
  - Large touch targets
  - Clear typography
  - Predictable navigation patterns
- Early feedback was incorporated before visual styling began

---

## Usability Testing

Moderated usability tests were conducted using mid-fidelity prototypes.

### What We Tested
- Ease of navigation
- Clarity of labels and actions
- User understanding of logs and schedules

### Outcomes
- Identified friction in navigation depth
- Refined terminology to be more user-friendly
- Reduced cognitive load on key screens

---

## High-Fidelity Design

Post-testing, high-fidelity designs were created with:
- A consistent visual language aligned with ERbuddy branding
- Accessible color contrast and typography
- Clear visual hierarchy for critical information
- Reusable UI components documented in a style guide

---

## Key Features

- **Exercise Library**  
  A curated set of science-backed exercises to support patient health.

- **Patient Logbook**  
  Enables patients and caregivers to track updates, symptoms, and progress, with AI-driven suggestions based on data patterns.

- **Personalized Daily Schedule**  
  A patient-focused dashboard reflecting daily tasks, exercises, and reminders.

---

## Outcome & Impact

The redesigned ERbuddy experience improved clarity, usability, and confidence for both patients and caregivers. Stakeholders responded positively to the research-driven approach and the emphasis on accessibility and real-world caregiving needs.

This project demonstrates strong end-to-end UX practice, from research and systems thinking to high-fidelity execution in a complex healthcare context.
